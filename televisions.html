It’s a Friday night, and the Sharma family is gathered in their favorite electronics store, marveling at the dazzling wall of televisions. Young Aryan, fascinated by the colossal 85-inch OLED display, tugs at his dad’s sleeve.

His father chuckles but hesitates. “It’s definitely impressive, but have you thought about how much power it will consume?”


Aryan’s mom, an energy-conscious consumer, pulls out her phone and starts comparing the energy ratings. “A bigger screen often means higher power consumption,” she explains. “But let’s see what the data says.”

Why Does it Matter?


With rising electricity costs and growing environmental concerns, understanding how screen size impacts power usage is crucial. While a larger screen provides a better viewing experience, it may also come with a hidden cost—higher energy consumption. The question is: Is there a trade-off between entertainment and sustainability?
