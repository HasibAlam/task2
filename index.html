Australia's Energy Consumption

Australia’s energy consumption rose 2.0% in 2022–23 to 5,882 petajoules, an increase of 117 petajoules.

This follows three successive years of decline, with consumption still not back to pre-pandemic levels.

Fossil fuels (coal, oil, and gas) accounted for 91% of Australia’s primary energy mix in 2022-23.

Oil accounted for the largest share at 39%, followed by coal and gas both at 26%.

Renewable energy sources accounted for only 9%.

source : Energy Gov Australia
